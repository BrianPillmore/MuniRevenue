## 2. Value Proposition by Role

### Mayor / Commission Chair
> "Find missing revenue you didn't know you were owed — without adding staff or changing how you work with OkTAP."

Pain: Mayors are accountable for the budget but lack tools to monitor it between monthly reports. They don't know what they're missing until the annual audit.

MuniRev gives them a clear answer: here are the businesses that filed last month but not this month. Here's how much that probably represents. Here's the trend.

### Finance Director / Treasurer
> "Automated anomaly alerts replace the manual spreadsheet review you're doing every month — and catch things spreadsheets miss."

Pain: Finance Directors pull OkTAP reports manually, export to Excel, and eyeball for problems. It takes hours and misses subtle multi-month patterns.

MuniRev automates the pull, flags deviations from statistical baselines, and surfaces missed filers before the revenue gap compounds.

### City Manager / Administrator
> "Show your council a data-backed revenue forecast — not a number from last year plus three percent."

Pain: Budget presentations rely on historical averages and gut feel. Councils increasingly push back without supporting data.

MuniRev generates 12-month rolling forecasts by tax type, benchmarked against historical actuals, ready to drop into a council presentation.

## 3. Key Pain Points Addressed

**1. Missed filings = undetected revenue loss**
Businesses that stop filing don't send a notification. They just disappear from the report. A mid-sized city with 800 active sales tax filers may have 20–50 lapsed filers in any given month. At average contribution levels, that's $10K–$500K+ per year in revenue that could be recovered through follow-up with the Oklahoma Tax Commission.

**2. Manual OkTAP review is slow and error-prone**
OkTAP exports XML-formatted reports that require manual processing. Staff spend hours each month on a task that should take minutes. Errors compound — a missed anomaly in month 3 may not surface until the annual audit.

**3. No proactive alerting**
There is no native alert in OkTAP when revenue is 20% below forecast. Cities find out when the monthly report comes in, and only if someone notices.

**4. No peer benchmarking**
Finance Directors have no way to know whether their revenue growth is keeping pace with comparable cities. MuniRev can eventually provide this, and the framing is immediately compelling.

**5. Budget forecasting by intuition**
Most cities still forecast by taking last year's number and applying a fixed growth rate. Statistical time-series forecasting (which MuniRev provides) catches seasonal patterns, trend breaks, and industry shifts that gut-feel methods miss.

## 6. Free Access Strategy

### MuniRev is Free — No Catches, No Expiration

The fastest path to adoption is removing every barrier. MuniRev is free for all Oklahoma cities and counties during the launch phase. No trial period, no credit card, no "pilot cohort" limit.

**Why free first:**
- Municipal procurement is slow. A free tool bypasses the budget approval process entirely — a finance director or mayor can just start using it.
- The data speaks for itself. Once a city sees their own missed filers and forecasts, they're sold. No sales call needed.
- Word spreads at OML events. "We've been using this for free and it found us $80K in missed filings" is the best marketing possible.
- Building the user base and proving value across diverse city sizes generates the evidence needed to justify any future pricing conversation.

### What "Free" Includes

Every city and county gets full access to:
- **Missed filing detection** — monthly report of businesses that went dark
- **Revenue forecasting** — 12-month rolling forecast by tax type
- **Anomaly alerts** — automatic flags when revenue deviates from baseline
- **NAICS industry breakdown** — see which sectors are driving or dragging revenue
- **PDF exports** — council-ready reports, no design work required

### Future Monetization (Not Now)

Free is the strategy today. When the time is right — after meaningful adoption, proven outcomes, and city leadership asking for advanced features — a premium tier can be introduced without disrupting existing free users. The path is: **free → habit → indispensable → paid upgrade for power features**.

No pricing decisions will be made until there are at least 25 active city users.

## 9. Brian's Unique Advantage

### Peer-to-Peer Credibility at Scale

Brian is not a vendor calling on a mayor. He is a mayor calling on a mayor. This distinction matters enormously in the municipal world, where trust is built through shared experience and skepticism of outside vendors is high. A cold call from a SaaS company gets ignored. A direct note from another sitting Oklahoma mayor gets read.

### Uses MuniRev for Yukon — Not Vaporware

Yukon is the reference account. Brian can say with specificity: "When I ran this on Yukon's data, here is what I found." He can show the actual dashboard, the actual reports, the actual numbers. No prospect has to take a leap of faith on whether the product works.

### Oklahoma Municipal League (OML) Access

The OML Annual Conference is the single highest-leverage event on the calendar. Every mayor, city manager, and finance director in the state attends or sends a representative. Options:

- **Session proposal**: Submit a session titled "Using Data to Find Revenue You're Missing" for the 2026 conference. Brian presents as Mayor of Yukon with MuniRev as the case study.
- **Exhibitor booth**: Reserve a booth in the exhibitor hall. Run live demos using attendees' own city data.
- **Lunch roundtable**: Host a working lunch for 10–15 finance directors. Frame it as peer learning, not a sales pitch.
- **Pre-conference outreach**: Use OML membership directory to reach all attendees with a "meet me at the conference" email 2 weeks before.

The OML conference should be treated as a launch event. All pilot slots that have not been filled by the conference date should be offered at the booth.

### Credibility Transfer

When Brian signs Tier 1 clients, they become implicit references for Tier 2 and 3 outreach. The social proof chain: "The City of Norman uses MuniRev" closes deals with Chickasha faster than any email template.

## 10. Sponsorship / Revenue Model

Since the platform is free for cities, the revenue model is sponsor-supported. A single tasteful banner ad from a relevant Oklahoma sponsor covers operating costs and keeps the service free indefinitely.

### Ideal Sponsor Profile

Sponsors should be companies that already serve Oklahoma municipalities and want visibility with mayors, finance directors, and county commissioners. The audience is small but high-value.

**Best-fit categories:**
- **Municipal banking** — BancFirst, MidFirst, ONB (Oklahoma National Bank), First National Bank — all have municipal deposit relationships
- **Bond counsel / municipal finance** — law firms and financial advisors who underwrite city debt
- **Audit firms** — CPA firms that do municipal audits (Eide Bailly, KPMG, RSM)
- **Municipal software** — Tyler Technologies, OpenGov, Caselle (non-competing products like utility billing, permitting, HR)
- **Insurance** — Oklahoma Municipal Assurance Group (OMAG), the primary OK municipal insurer — already OML-adjacent
- **Infrastructure / engineering** — firms that bid on city projects (traffic, water, roads)

### Sponsorship Pitch

> "MuniRev reaches every mayor, finance director, and city manager in Oklahoma who cares about their revenue. Our users are the exact decision-makers who approve municipal banking, bonding, auditing, and vendor contracts. One banner placement, one audience."

**Sponsorship tiers (suggested, negotiable):**
| Tier | Placement | Impressions (est.) | Monthly Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Presenting Sponsor | Banner on all dashboard pages + PDF reports | All active users | $1,000–$2,500/mo |
| Supporting Sponsor | Banner on reports only | Report-view users | $500–$1,000/mo |

**Start with OMAG** — they're OML-connected, already known to municipal officials, and have clear incentive to be in front of city finance staff monthly.

### Sponsor Implementation

A non-intrusive banner in the dashboard header or report footer:
- 728×90 leaderboard or 300×90 slim banner
- "Presented by [Sponsor]" label with logo and link
- No tracking pixels or third-party scripts that could create privacy concerns for government users
- Sponsor gets a monthly impressions report (anonymized — no city names shared)
